Self-Assessment Quiz “I say yes to a person when I want to say no.” Yes, I do that often. I say no and tell people why I’m saying no so that they don’t ask me anymore. I usually say no without apologizing or lying about it. “I feel like I’m constantly having to save people close to me and fix their problems.” No, I don’t get involved in other people’s issues. Yes, often. No, I know my limitations and offer what I can, when I can. “I regularly find myself sucked into pointless fighting or debating.” Yes. No, I don’t experience that. No, I try to keep people at a distance. “I loan money to friends or family out of feelings of pity, guilt, obligation, or being threatened.” Yes. No, I loan money with clear expectations of when I’d like to be paid back. No, I don’t trust people and/or I want to hold on to every penny I earn. “I frequently feel stressed about work.” Yes. No, I never think about work. When I’m off, I check out and don’t care what happens. No, I turn off work notifications and ignore calls, texts, or emails. I try to be present with my friends/family/self once I’m home. There are some situations when I might be a little flexible (in case of emergencies or during a big project), but I make sure I don’t get sucked into it for too long. “I think I spend too much time on social media.” Yes. No, I enjoy checking in on social media once in a while but don’t get lost in it. I log on to my social media account for work on specific days and times. Then I log out of the app on my phone. “I feel guilty when I say no to someone.” Yes. No. No, I don’t care what people think. I’m annoyed/angry/frustrated when they ask me for things. “I get roped into activities or obligations that I don’t want to do.” Yes. No. No, people know not to ask me to do things. “I don’t trust people.” True. Not true of all people. No, I trust everyone, and sometimes that causes problems for me. “I share too much personal information too soon.” Yes. No. I don’t trust people enough to share personal information. “I’m able to hear no from people and not take it personally.” Yes. No, I tend to take it personally. I don’t usually ask people for help. I don’t think they’ll do it right, or I can’t trust them. “I fail to speak up when I’m treated badly.” True. No, I cut people off, I cuss people out, or I gossip about them. No, I’m able to tell people how I feel. “I feel guilty for dedicating time to myself.” No, I know I need to take care of myself in order to take care of others. “You can’t pour from an empty cup.” Yes. No, I prioritize my own needs over everyone else’s. “I apologize for things when they’re not my fault.” No, things aren’t typically my fault. Yes. No, I apologize when I’ve done something I’m responsible for and know I’ve hurt someone. “I feel scattered and stressed because I have a million things to do and not enough time.” Yes. Honestly, I don’t have a lot going on. Life is quiet because I don’t have a lot of friends or commitments. No, I’ve learned to say no, outsource, delegate, or ask for help to avoid feeling scattered or stressed. “I don’t speak up when I have something important to share.” Yes. No, I know that my ideas and input are just as important as everyone else’s. No, in fact, I find myself silencing others at work or not giving them a chance to speak up.
